Crawlers Turn 50, Pivot Toward Future Exploration

February 23, 2015

NASA's crawler-transporters, two of the largest vehicles ever built, have carried NASA rockets and spacecraft to the launch pad for the last 50 years. They will continue their legacy as the "workhorses" of the nation's space program as part of the agency's journey to Mars. Originally constructed in 1965 to support the agency's Apollo Program, the crawlers are being modified to carry NASA's Space Launch System (SLS) with the Orion spacecraft atop it and potential commercial vehicles to their pads to begin space exploration missions.

February 23, 2015

Wilmore and Virts completed all the scheduled tasks for today and one get ahead task.
